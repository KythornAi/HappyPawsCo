## Core Principles

### 1. Brand Voice
- Warm and conversational (like a knowledgeable friend chatting at the park)
- Empathetic to pet parent struggles
- Expert without being preachy
- Light humour where appropriate
- UK-focused (weather, regulations, culture)
- **Relatable, not robotic** - avoid corporate/salesy language
- **Conversational social proof** - "speaking with friends", "reviews I've read", not "customers tell us"

## Product Positioning

### Rule: Never Cannibalize Other Products

When writing copy for similar products, frame each for its **specific use case** without dismissing alternatives.

**Example: Multiple Harnesses**

**Crash-tested harness:**
- Persona: Safety-conscious, possibly new pet parent
- Frame: "For car journeys, the crash-tested design gives peace of mind. Easy to use for walks too, but if you're just out for a stroll without driving, the regular harnesses are brilliant."

**Standard reflective harnesses:**
- Persona: Everyday walker, younger/untrained dogs
- Frame: Great for everyday walks, front D-ring works with dual-clip lead for no-pull training
- âŒ Never say "budget-conscious" or "cheaper option"
- âœ… Highlight: Everyday use, training compatibility, visibility

**Premium harness (6-point adjustment):**
- Persona: Trained dogs, comfort-focused owners
- Frame: 6 points of adjustment for custom fit, suited for dogs who've mastered walking
- âŒ Never say "premium" or "more expensive"
- âœ… Highlight: Adjustability, comfort, control

## Pain Point Variation

For similar products, **vary the opening pain point** to differentiate without confusing.

**Example: 3 Reflective Harnesses**

**Harness A intro:** Lead with pulling/training challenge
**Harness B intro:** Lead with visibility/safety in dark
**Harness C intro:** Lead with comfort/fit concerns

All three still mention other benefits in body copy, but the **hook** differs.

**Goal:** Subtle differentiation that helps customers choose, without creating "analysis paralysis."

## Preserved Elements (Do NOT Touch)

### Must Preserve Exactly:

1. **Safety notes, reassurance sections, disclaimers**
   - "Reassurance: Designed for small-to-medium dogs up to 11kg..."
   - "Safety note: This harness is designed for walking and training..."
   - "Note: 'Crash-tested' refers to manufacturer testing methods..."

2. **"Pairs well with:" sections**
   - These are already optimized
   - Do NOT add or remove items
   - Keep links exactly as they are

3. **Variant references**
   - "Prefer a black finish? See the black version here."
   - "Need a different size? Shop Size L."

4. **Size specifications, care instructions**
   - Keep measurements exact
   - Preserve washing/care guidance

5. **Hygiene notices**
   - "Sealed items are non-returnable once opened (unless faulty)."

## UK Context Integration

## Conversion Psychology Elements

### Trust Signals
- Social proof (customers, reviews, friends)
- Safety reassurance (preserved sections)
- Clear sizing guidance
- Care instructions (shows product longevity)

### Objection Handling
- Address fit concerns (measurement guide)
- Address durability ("tough enough for daily walks")
- Address ease of use ("even wriggly dogs can't escape")

### Urgency (Gentle)
- âŒ No "limited time" or "act now" pressure
