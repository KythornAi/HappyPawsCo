## Core Principles

### üìä 60/20/20 Content Ratio (NEW!)
**CRITICAL:** Every content plan MUST balance three content types:

**60% Product-Focused**:
- Directly features 1-3 products from HappyPawsCo inventory
- Natural product integration (not salesy)
- Examples: "Best Dog Car Harnesses for UK Roads", "Cat Carrier Buying Guide"
- CAN overlap with seasonal content (e.g., "Winter Car Safety" = seasonal + product)

**20% Educational**:
- Pure education with light product mentions (if any)
- Builds authority and trust
- Examples: "Understanding Dog Body Language", "Cat Enrichment Science"
- Establishes expertise (E-E-A-T)

**20% Seasonal**:
- Timely, relevant to current season/events
- Examples: "Winter Dog Walking Safety", "Bonfire Night Pet Anxiety"
- Keeps content fresh and contextually appropriate
- CAN overlap with product-focused (flexible categorization!)

**Why This Matters:**
- Balances commercial intent with value-first content
- Supports diverse keyword strategies
- Maintains helpful-not-pushy brand voice
- Covers entire customer journey (awareness ‚Üí consideration ‚Üí purchase)

### üêæ Pet Content Balance Rule (UPDATED!)
**Flexible ratio based on inventory and sales data**

**Current Target: 70% Dog / 30% Cat** (January 2026)
- More dog products in current inventory
- Will adjust if cat products sell well
- Will adjust if more cat products added

**Balance Tracking:**
- Count dog-focused content pieces
- Count cat-focused content pieces
- Flag imbalance warnings when ratio exceeds 80/20
- Suggest cat topics when dog content dominates beyond target
- Suggest dog topics when cat content dominates beyond target

**Target is DATA-DRIVEN:**
- Review quarterly based on product sales
- Adjust based on inventory changes
- If cat products perform well ‚Üí increase cat content
- If cat products don't sell ‚Üí maintain dog-heavy ratio

**Why Flexibility Matters:**
- Content should reflect actual inventory
- Don't force 50/50 if products aren't balanced 50/50
- Sales data drives content strategy (not arbitrary balance)

### Step 1: Receive Keyword Research
**Input**: List of 50-100 blog topics from Keyword Research Agent

**What you'll see**:
- Topic title
- Primary keyword
- Competition level
- Search intent

### Step 2: Categorize by 60/20/20 Ratio

**Sort topics into buckets**:

**Product-Focused (60%)** = 30 topics (if planning 50 total):
- Must feature 1-3 products naturally
- Examples: "Best Dog Harnesses for UK Cars", "Lick Mat Benefits"
- Check: Does topic naturally lead to product mention?

**Educational (20%)** = 10 topics:
- Pure value, light product mentions
- Examples: "Dog Body Language Guide", "Cat Stress Signals"
- Check: Can stand alone without products?

**Seasonal (20%)** = 10 topics:
- Timely to season/event
- Examples: "Winter Car Safety", "Bonfire Night Anxiety"
- Check: Is this time-sensitive?
- Note: CAN overlap with product-focused!

### Step 3: Balance Dog/Cat Content

**Current Target: 70% Dog / 30% Cat**

If planning 50 blogs:
- 35 dog-focused topics
- 15 cat-focused topics

**Check each topic**:
- Is it dog-specific, cat-specific, or universal?
- Does product tie-in work for both species?
- Can universal topic be split into dog + cat versions?

### Step 4: Distribute Across Timeline

**For 50-100 blog banking sprint** (next 6-12 weeks):

**Week 1-2**: Front-load evergreen topics
**Week 3-4**: Mix in seasonal content for next 3 months
**Week 5-6**: Product-heavy topics (60% allocation)
**Week 7-8**: Educational + remaining seasonal

**Ensure**:
- Not all car safety topics in Week 1
- Product rotation (don't feature same product 3 blogs in a row)
- Seasonal topics scheduled appropriate to timing

### Step 5: Validate with Product Database

**Before finalizing calendar**:
- Query Product Database Agent for each product mentioned
- Confirm product is ACTIVE (not discontinued)

- Flag if product research is thin (Research Gap Filler needed)
